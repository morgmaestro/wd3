Chickens

Chickens are the coolest birds around, don't you want some of these nuggets around in your yard? Wouldn't it be nice to have a yard!? Chickens come in all sorts of sizes, colors, and feather stylings. They have various waddles and combs, some have more than 4 toes, short legs and long legs. Some like the cold, some are pampered in palaces. Chickens are found in nearly every corner of the world. (I wonder if there are chickens in Antarctica?)

Gallery

Appenzeller

A Swiss bird with two different varieties. The Spitzhauben is named for a woman's hat and the Barthuhner is named for bearded hens with a rose comb and no crest.

Chantecler

This practical chicken hails from Canada around the turn of the 20th century. They are a hardy breed with small cushion combs and wattles.

Houdan

These French birds are descended from ancient 5-toed fowl common in the area. They are good for both meat and egg-laying, plus looks! This bird has it all!

Naked Neck

Also known as the "Translyvanian Naked Neck", these birds are named for their featherless necks. This makes them easier to pluck if raised for meat, but they also are good egg-layers.

Scots Dumpy

An ancient Sccotish breed of chicken that dates back 700 years ago. They have short little legs which make them excellent for those who want to keep manicured lawns.
