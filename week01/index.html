about

Hannah Hillam is a San Francisco-based artist whose cynaical and surreal comics have gone viral on instagram. Most of her followers are women ages 20-40 who share her awkward spin on life. Her artwork is inspired by her every day life, living with mental illness, and the world around her. One of her most common subjects is an alternate reality where she has become her cat's pet. She credits a moment of success in the art industry when her grandparents started taking her seriously.

gallery

Hillam sums up her style as "Unsettlingly cute. Unnervingly heartwarming." Her work is at both times relatable and surreal. She cites illustrators such as Arthur Rackham, Charles Dana Gibson, NC Wyeth as early inspiration, and follows currently working artists Jillian Tamaki, Yuko Shimizu, Noelle Stevenson, Kate Beaton, James Jean, Shen Comix, Julia Kaye, and Safely Endangered.
